About tour

Join a tour of Paris to explore some of its iconic landmarks, rich history, and diverse culture. We'll begin at the Eiffel Tower to marvel at the panoramic views of the city. Then, we'll visit the Notre Dame Cathedral, stroll down the Champs-Élysées, and pay our respects at the Arc de Triomphe. At the Louvre Museum, we'll admire the incredible art pieces, including the famous Mona Lisa. And, we'll indulge in Paris's famous cuisine. Finally, we'll take a romantic boat ride down the Seine River to see the city's beauty from a new perspective. More [+]

Itinerary

  • Visit the Eiffel Tower and take a tour to the top of the tower for panoramic views of Paris. Explore the Champs-Elysées avenue, known for its high-end fashion boutiques, cafes and cinemas. Stroll through the Jardin des Tuileries, a beautiful park located between the Louvre Museum and the Place de la Concorde. Have dinner in Montmartre, a picturesque neighborhood famous for its bohemian charm and artists' studios.

  • Spend the day at the Louvre Museum, home to famous works of art such as the Mona Lisa, the Winged Victory of Samothrace, and the Venus de Milo. Visit the nearby Sainte Chapelle, a Gothic chapel known for its stained glass windows. Take a stroll along the Seine River and cross over to the Left Bank to explore the Latin Quarter, known for its historic universities, bookstores, and cafes.

  • Visit Notre-Dame Cathedral, a historic Gothic cathedral known for its stunning architecture and religious significance. Walk through the charming streets of the Marais neighborhood, known for its trendy shops, art galleries, and cafes. Explore the Centre Pompidou, a modern art museum and cultural center known for its avant-garde architecture and exhibits.

  • Take a day trip to the Palace of Versailles, a stunning royal chateau and UNESCO World Heritage site located just outside of Paris. Explore the vast gardens, the Hall of Mirrors, and the ornate apartments of the kings and queens of France.

  • Spend the morning at the Musée d'Orsay, a museum housed in a former train station and home to an extensive collection of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist art. Take a stroll through the Montparnasse neighborhood, known for its historic cafes, theaters, and art studios. End the day with a visit to the iconic Moulin Rouge, a cabaret known for its extravagant shows and historic significance.
